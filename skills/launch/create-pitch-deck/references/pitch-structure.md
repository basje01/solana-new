# Pitch Structure

Slide-by-slide framework for crypto project pitches.

## Core Slides (10-12 slides total)

### 1. Title Slide
- Project name and one-liner
- Your name and role
- One visual that captures the essence

### 2. Problem
- What specific problem are you solving?
- Who has this problem? (Be specific — not "everyone")
- What's the current workaround? How painful is it?
- Use a concrete story or data point

### 3. Solution
- What do you do, in one sentence?
- How does it work? (3 bullet points max)
- Screenshot or demo GIF

### 4. Why Crypto / Why Solana
- What's impossible without a blockchain?
- Why Solana specifically? (speed, cost, ecosystem, composability)
- This is the slide where you prove crypto necessity, not just assert it

### 5. Demo / Product
- Live demo link (devnet is fine for hackathons, mainnet for VCs)
- 3-4 screenshots showing the core flow
- Key metrics: transactions, users, volume (if any)

### 6. Market
- Who is your target user? (specific persona)
- How big is the addressable market? (bottom-up, not top-down)
- What's your wedge? (the small market you'll own first)

### 7. Traction
- Users, transactions, TVL, revenue — whatever you have
- Growth rate matters more than absolute numbers for early stage
- If pre-launch: waitlist, LOIs, partnerships, community size

### 8. Business Model
- How will you make money?
- Unit economics (or plan to figure them out)
- Token model (if applicable) — be honest about value capture

### 9. Competition
- Landscape matrix (from competitive-landscape skill if available)
- Your unfair advantage
- Why now? What changed that makes this possible today?

### 10. Team
- Why is this the right team to build this?
- Relevant experience (crypto, domain, technical)
- Advisors and backers (if notable)

### 11. Ask
- What do you need? (funding amount, partnerships, users, feedback)
- What will you do with it? (next 6 months roadmap)
- Specific, concrete ask — not "we're raising"

### 12. Contact
- How to reach you
- Links: website, GitHub, Twitter, Discord
