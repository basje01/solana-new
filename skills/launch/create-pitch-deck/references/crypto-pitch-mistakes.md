# Crypto Pitch Mistakes

Common antipatterns that kill crypto pitches.

## Mistake 1: Leading with Technology
- Bad: "We built a ZK-compressed state channel on Solana"
- Good: "Freelancers lose 5% on every cross-border payment. We fix that."
- Rule: Problem first, technology second

## Mistake 2: Vague Market Sizing
- Bad: "The DeFi market is $50B"
- Good: "12,000 Solana DAOs need treasury management. 200 are actively looking."
- Rule: Bottom-up TAM from real users, not top-down from CoinGecko

## Mistake 3: No Crypto Necessity
- Bad: "We're building Uber but on the blockchain"
- Good: "Cross-border agent payments need settlement in <1 second for <$0.01. Only Solana delivers both."
- Rule: If removing the blockchain makes the product better, you have a problem

## Mistake 4: Token Without Utility
- Bad: "Our token will appreciate as the network grows"
- Good: "Stakers earn 3% of protocol fees. Current APY: 12%."
- Rule: If the token is just for speculation, say so or remove it

## Mistake 5: Ignoring Competition
- Bad: "We have no competitors"
- Good: "Marinade and Jito dominate liquid staking, but neither serves institutional stakers. That's our wedge."
- Rule: Acknowledging competition shows maturity

## Mistake 6: No Demo
- Bad: "We'll have a beta in Q3"
- Good: [live demo link] "Try it yourself — here's a devnet wallet with test SOL"
- Rule: Working code > slides

## Mistake 7: Vanity Metrics
- Bad: "10,000 Twitter followers"
- Good: "340 weekly active users, 15% week-over-week growth"
- Rule: Usage metrics > attention metrics

## Mistake 8: No Clear Ask
- Bad: "We're looking for strategic partners"
- Good: "Raising $500K pre-seed to hire 2 engineers and launch mainnet by June"
- Rule: Specific amount, specific use, specific timeline
